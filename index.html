What & where?

USGS identifies the site as Madera Quarry (past producer) with stated coordinates and PLSS location in the W½ of Sec. 16. The quarry label appears on USGS topos near Adkins Mountain, just north of the historic Bates Station locality.

Primary source: Logan’s Mines and Mineral Resources of Madera County (Oct 1950) describes the opening by Kingsland Granite Co., the Madera cutting plant (lathe + 3 gang saws), the 1937 name change, and the 1942 wind‑down.1

Overview

In 1934, the Kingsland Granite Company opened a granite quarry in Sec. 16, T9S, R20E (MDM), “north of Bates,” about 20 miles by road northeast of Madera. Blocks were trucked to a cutting and finishing plant in Madera equipped with a large lathe, three gang saws, carborundum and surfacing machines “to prepare granite for a large building project.” In May 1937 the corporation name changed to Madera Quarries Company. Granite was marketed for building and monumental use from 1938–1942; no production was reported after 1942, and the company withdrew as a California corporation on July 30, 1946.1 was marketed from 1938 through 1942 for building and monumental use; no production was reported after 1942, and the company withdrew as a California corporation on July 30, 1946.1

USGS’s MRDS corroborates the site as a past producer with discovery and first production in 1934 and ownership by Kingsland Granite Co. (1934) and Madera Quarries Co. (1937).2 Mindat reproduces the same location and timeline.3

Note on the “large building project”: Period ads show Kingsland Granite Company promoting “Kingsland Gray for the Federal Office Building, San Francisco,” i.e., today’s 50 United Nations Plaza (constructed 1934–36). This is an advertisement, not a contract ledger, but it aligns with Logan’s 1934–37 production narrative.4, 5

Timeline

1934 — Quarry opened by Kingsland Granite Co.; blocks hauled to Madera finishing plant (lathe + 3 gang saws).1
1935–1936 — Active marketing as “Kingsland Gray,” including the San Francisco Federal Office Building claim.4
May 1937 — Corporate name change to Madera Quarries Co.1
1938–1942 — Granite marketed; last reported production in 1942.1
1940 — Directory listing: “Madera Quarries Co., Colma — RA ndolph‑8482,” evidencing a Bay Area yard/office.6
July 30, 1946 — Company withdrew as a California corporation.1
1950 — Logan’s county report publishes the summary and mine‑list entry: “Operated 1938–42 by Madera Quarries Co. (withdrawn 1946).”1
